Quantum Computing is taking the world by storm. It can reduce problems that require exponential time to polynomial time problems. In Quantum computers, the state of the system is encoded in units called qubits. Ion traps are one of the many methods to build a qubit. It traps a single Calcium ion in a dynamic electric field. The steps to build an ion trap are:

  • Build a vacuum chamber which can hold 10-10 Torr
  • Evacuate the chamber of all particles using turbopumps, ton pumps and baking
  • Activate the Paul trap in the vacuum and shoot Calcium ions to be trapped
  • Perform quantum gate operations on this ion using a laser

The experimental setup is extremely sensitive. Even a single stray speck of dust can throw the system into disarray. The video above shows an experiment by Caltech trapping pollen with a Paul trap.
