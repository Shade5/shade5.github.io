eLISA Gravitational Wave Detector

The evolved laser Interferometer space antenna is a mission proposed by the European Space Agency. It will be used to detect and measure low frequency gravitational waves in the range of 10-5 Hz to 1 Hz. The mission is set to be launched in 2034, but a forerunner mission, the LISA Pathfinder was launched on December 3 2015 to test the technologies that are planned for eLISA. The interactive plot above is the estimated path of the space-crafts around the sun.

Three space-crafts follow a Helio-centric orbit around the sun. They form an equilateral triangle with an arm length in the order of one million km. The interferometer requires the space-crafts to maintain constant distance between them.
This plot measures the oscillation in the distances between the spacecrafts, for all initial phases.

Clearly a global minimum is observer, which indicates the ideal orbit for the spacecrafts.
